The Alaska Milk Run is a series of flights that connects major cities (Seattle and Anchorage) with smaller communities. Each route is flown with the same aircraft the entire way, with takeoff and landing at each stop along the way.

Besides offering rural towns and communities a way to get supplies and travel (as the only way out in some towns are through these flights), they also make great sight-seeing flights. These Milk Run flights are popular among avgeeks for its unique service.

Why this site?

Both the Alaska Milk Run and United Island Hopper are flights sought after by avgeeks for their uniqueness and incredible views. However, booking these flights on their respective airline pages (or using Google Flights) can be cumbersome as multi-city bookings require manual entry for each origin, destination, and date. The time spent booking flights can add up while researching multiple dates or flight paths.

This site aims to simplify the process of researching these unique flights by generating direct links to the airline booking pages with simpler options. The website also has persistent storage for saved links, allowing users to easily check for price changes or schedule changes before booking.
